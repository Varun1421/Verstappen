Dominance Redefined

Visualizing Max Verstappen's 2023 Formula 1 Season

Varun Brahma

Fans remember the 2023 season as Verstappen's most dominant. This project turns that memory into measurement: how dominance built race by race, how much was driver vs. car, where it sits in F1 history, and how it compares to peak performances in other sports.

Objective 3 — F1 History

Where 2023 Ranks Among Dominant Seasons

Ascari 1952, Clark 1963, Senna 1988, Schumacher 2004, Vettel 2013, Hamilton 2020 — each remembered as historically dominant. Every axis below is normalized to a percentage so eras with different scoring systems and season lengths are directly comparable. A line that hugs the top of every axis is the most dominant. Hover any line to isolate it. The legend mirrors the hover state so the highlighted season is easier to identify.

Rate metrics Wins, podiums, poles and fastest laps are all converted to percentages of races entered.
Era adjustment Points Capture normalizes scoring systems by comparing average points to the maximum available per race.
Reading the plot A season that stays high across all axes was not only fast, but also consistent and reliable.

Win Rate, Podium Rate, Pole Rate and Fastest Lap Rate are computed as counts ÷ races. Points Capture is average points-per-race ÷ the maximum points possible per race in that era. Reliability is 1 − (DNFs ÷ races).

Objective 4 — Cross-Sport Greats

A Dominance Curve You Can Compare

For every athlete in their peak season, this plot shows the cumulative fraction of events in which they finished at or better than each rank: race finish in F1, tournament result in tennis and golf, and medal-race result in swimming. A curve that jumps to 1.0 by position 1 means they won every event. The further a curve sits to the upper-left, the more dominant the season.

Common unit Every sport is reduced to event finishing rank, so first place means a win regardless of the sport's format.
Curve height The y-value answers: what share of events were this good or better?
Curve shape Earlier jumps mean more wins and podium-level finishes concentrated at the very front.

F1 finishing positions for Verstappen 2023 are exact (from the season results CSV). Distributions for Tiger Woods 2000 and Djokovic 2015 are approximate but consistent with public season-end totals (wins, finals reached, top-10s). Federer 2006 and Phelps 2008 are exact (12 titles / 16 finals / 17 SFs+; 8-for-8 Olympic golds).
